# AI Detection Checklist

All documentation shipped by Light Architects must pass this checklist. Written artifacts should read like they were written by the person who built the thing, not generated by a language model.

## Linguistic Markers (High Confidence)

Flag and rewrite if any of these appear:

1. **Formulaic openers**: "In this document", "A [adjective] [noun] that", "This [noun] provides", "Here we present"
2. **Hedging filler**: "approximately" (use "about" or the exact number), "it is important to note", "it should be noted", "it's worth mentioning"
3. **Template phrases**: "This work builds on", "We acknowledge", "In conclusion", "Key takeaway", "As mentioned above", "Without further ado"
4. **PAQ words**: "delve into", "at its core", "a testament to", "serves as a", "plays a crucial role", "in the realm of", "leverage" (as a verb)
5. **Corporate softening**: "We are pleased to announce", "Going forward", "At this juncture", "With that being said"
6. **Sweeping generalizations**: "All prior work", "Every existing solution", "No other approach", "The first to ever"
7. **Editorializing disguised as neutrality**: "On the one hand... on the other hand", "While some may argue"

## Structural Markers (Medium Confidence)

Flag if pattern appears more than the threshold:

8. **Em dashes**: More than 1 per 500 words. Replace with periods, commas, or parentheses.
9. **Bullet list dominance**: More than 3 consecutive bulleted sections without prose paragraphs between them.
10. **Bold overuse**: Bold text in more than 20% of paragraphs (excluding headers).
11. **Uniform paragraph structure**: Every paragraph opens with a topic sentence and closes with a summary. Real writing varies.
12. **Repetitive sentence patterns**: Same construction ("X is Y. Z is W. A is B.") in consecutive sentences.
13. **"Approximately" count**: More than 1 occurrence in any document. Pick "about" or the exact number.
14. **Consistent sentence length**: Standard deviation of sentence length below 5 words across 10+ sentences.

## Content Markers (Low Confidence)

Flag but require human judgment:

15. **Numbers in multiple files with different values**: Cross-reference README, JSON, logs, code.
16. **Claims without evidence**: "Provably optimal" without a citation or proof sketch.
17. **Passive voice over 30%**: Count passive constructions. Technical writing allows some; 30%+ is a flag.
18. **Perfectly polished grammar**: Zero typos, zero informal constructions, zero contractions. Real humans use contractions and occasionally break grammar rules for emphasis.

## Replacement Principles

When rewriting flagged content:

- **Lead with what it does, not what it is.** "Tiny model, big cache." not "A cache-first submission with a novel blending strategy."
- **Use the specific number, not the hedge.** "33ms per step" not "approximately 33 milliseconds per step."
- **Drop preambles.** Start lists and lineage sections directly. No "The following section describes..."
- **Write like you're explaining to a peer.** Direct, specific, conversational. Not formal, not casual. The voice of someone who built the thing and is telling you how it works.
- **Vary sentence structure.** Mix short declarative sentences with longer explanatory ones. Start some sentences with "But", "And", "So" when it reads naturally.
- **Use contractions.** "It doesn't" not "It does not" (unless emphasis requires the full form).
- **One number, one source.** Every metric traces to a single authoritative file. If README says 0.0381 and the log says 0.0380, one of them is wrong.
